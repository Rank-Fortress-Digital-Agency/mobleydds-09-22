THE TOP BENEFITS OF COSMETIC DENTISTRY


Most people know the importance of having a good oral hygiene routine. But what they may not know is that there are cosmetic dentistry procedures available that can improve their smile by leaps and bounds. Here are the top benefits of cosmetic dentistry:

Teeth Whitening:


One of the most popular cosmetic dentistry procedures is teeth whitening, which can dramatically improve the look of your smile. Teeth whitening can be done in a dentist’s office, or at home using over-the-counter products.

If you are considering teeth whitening, it is important to talk to your dentist first. They can help you decide if teeth whitening is right for you and determine the best way to achieve the results you desire.

There are two main types of teeth whitening products: bleaching agents and non-bleaching agents. Bleaching agents work by oxidizing the stains on your teeth, while non-bleaching agents simply work to remove surface stains.

In-office teeth whitening is usually done using a bleaching agent, such as hydrogen peroxide. Your dentist will apply the bleaching agent to your teeth and then use a special light to accelerate the whitening process.

Teeth whitening is generally safe for most people. However, there are some risks associated with the use of bleaching agents. These include tooth sensitivity and irritation of the gums. Be sure to talk to your dentist about these risks before you begin any teeth whitening treatment.

Bonding:


If you have chipped, cracked or discolored teeth, bonding can give you a beautiful new smile. Bonding is a conservative dental procedure that can be used to change the shape, color, and length of your teeth.

Bonding can be used to:

  • Close spaces between teeth
  • Make teeth look longer
  • Change the shape of teeth
  • Change the color of teeth

Bonding is usually done in one visit to the dentist. The procedure is not painful, but you may feel some pressure while the bonding material is being applied to your tooth. After the procedure, your tooth may be sensitive to hot and cold temperatures for a short time.

Bonding typically lasts for several years, but it can stain and chip like natural teeth. You will need to take care of your bonded teeth by brushing and flossing them daily and avoiding habits that can damage them, such as biting your nails or chewing on ice.

If you are considering bonding, talk to your dentist about whether this procedure is right for you.

Veneers:

Veneers are thin shells that are placed over your natural teeth to change their color or shape. They are custom-made to match your existing teeth and can give you a completely new look. Veneers can be made from porcelain or composite material, and each has its advantages. Porcelain veneers are more stain resistant than composite veneers and can give you a more natural-looking smile. However, they are also more expensive than composite veneers. Composite veneers are less expensive than porcelain veneers but may not last as long. Veneers are usually placed on the front teeth where they can be seen when you smile, but they can be placed on any tooth that needs to be repaired.

If you are considering getting veneers, it is important to consult with a qualified dentist to see if they are right for you. The dentist will examine your teeth and discuss your options with you. They will also take x-rays and impressions of your teeth to be sure that the veneers will fit properly. Veneers are usually not covered by dental insurance, so be sure to ask about the cost before you make a decision.

Veneers can give you a beautiful, new smile that you will be proud to show off. If you are considering this option, be sure to consult with a qualified dentist to ensure that they are right for you.

Invisalign:

Invisalign is a great alternative to traditional braces for people who want to straighten their teeth without anyone knowing. The clear aligners are virtually invisible and can be removed for eating and cleaning.

Invisalign is also more comfortable than traditional braces, because there are no wires or brackets that can rub against your gums and cause irritation.

If you are looking for a way to straighten your teeth without anyone knowing, Invisalign is a great option!

Dental Implants:

Dental implants are a great way to replace missing teeth and give you a natural-looking smile. They are also a permanent solution to tooth loss, which means you won’t have to worry about your implants coming loose or falling out. There are a few things you should know before getting dental implants, such as the different types of implants and the implant procedure.

There are two main types of dental implants: endosteal and subperiosteal. Endosteal implants are placed directly into the jawbone, while subperiosteal implants are placed on top of the jawbone under the gum tissue. Both types of implants are made of titanium, which is a strong and durable metal that can fuse to the bone.

The implant procedure begins with a small incision being made in the gums. A metal post, or abutment, is then placed into the incision. The implant itself is placed on top of the abutment. Once the implant is in place, the gums are stitched up and allowed to heal.

After the gums have healed, a dental prosthetic (false tooth) is attached to the implant. The prosthetic can be made from porcelain, ceramic, or metal. It will be color-matched to your natural teeth so that it blends in seamlessly.

Dental implants are a great way to achieve a natural-looking smile that is also permanent. With proper care, your implants can last a lifetime.

Tooth Reshaping:

If you have misshapen or uneven teeth, tooth reshaping can give you the beautiful smile you’ve always wanted. Also known as tooth contouring, tooth reshaping is a simple and quick cosmetic dentistry procedure that can be performed in just one visit to the dentist.

Tooth reshaping can correct several cosmetic dental concerns, such as:

  • Small chips or cracks in the teeth
  • Misshapen teeth
  • Teeth that are too pointy or sharp
  • Mild overcrowding
  • Uneven teeth (such as when one tooth sticks out further than the others)

It’s important to note that tooth reshaping is not meant to be a substitute for orthodontics (braces). If you have more serious dental issues that need to be addressed, such as severe overcrowding or misalignment, you will likely need to seek treatment from an orthodontist.

Gum Lift:

A gum lift is a procedure that removes excess gum tissue to reveal more of your natural teeth. This can help create a more balanced and symmetrical smile. It can also make your teeth look longer. A gum lift is also known as gingival depigmentation, gingival recontouring, or gingivectomy.

There are several reasons why you might need a gum lift. If your gums are uneven, it can affect the way your teeth look. You may have excess gum tissue that covers up part of your tooth, making it appear shorter than it is. Gum lifts can also be used to correct misaligned teeth or to prepare for dental implants.

The first step in getting a gum lift is to schedule a consultation with your dentist. During this visit, your dentist will examine your mouth to determine if you’re a good candidate for the procedure. They will also ask about your medical history and any medications you’re taking.

If you decide to move forward with a gum lift, the next step is to schedule the procedure. This is typically done as an outpatient procedure, which means you won’t have to stay overnight in the hospital. The procedure itself takes about an hour.

During a gum lift, your dentist will make small incisions in your gums. They will then remove excess gum tissue and contour your gums to give you the look you desire. In some cases, they may also need to remove a small amount of bone.

Smile Makeover:


A smile makeover is a combination of several different cosmetic dentistry procedures that are customized to meet your individual needs and goals. Whether you want whiter teeth, straighter teeth, or, a completely new smile, a smile makeover can give you the results you desire.

A smile makeover usually involves a combination of different cosmetic dentistry procedures. The most common procedures used in a smile makeover are teeth whitening, bonding, veneers, and gum contouring.

Teeth whitening is one of the simplest and most effective ways to improve the appearance of your teeth. There are two main types of teeth whitening: in-office teeth whitening and at-home teeth whitening. In-office teeth whitening is done by a dentist and takes about an hour. At-home teeth whitening takes a little longer, but you can do it in the comfort of your own home.

In Summary:


If you’re unhappy with your smile, cosmetic dentistry can help. These procedures can not only improve the look of your teeth, but also your self-confidence. To find out which cosmetic dentistry procedure is right for you, consult with a qualified and experienced dentist.
